Your liver produces ketones and you start burn stubborn fat.

As ketone level rise, you enter the healthy state of ketosis.

Most Commune Mistake On Keto

1. Being misguided

It is enough to type Keto diet in any search engine and loads of evidence with body transformation will pop up which will get you excited. Many people have been misguided by reading random articles from the internet, noted a few Keto recipes, started without following expert advice or plan and ended up in gaining weight, instead of losing it. We strongly recommend consulting an expert before starting.

2. Rushing

Most likely you have heard the stories: indeed, if you do the right thing, the Keto diet will melt 20 lbs or more per month. This nutritional technique has been used by thousands of men and women around the world. It has been demonstrated and the outcome speaks for itself. People are totally transformed whilst losing fat. Be alert though, you could end up worse off if you don't follow a fully qualified plan so its important not to rush.

3. Having too many carbs unintentionally

The great mystery behind the ketogenic diet at any age is ketosis, which is a state of extreme fat burning. Your body will use the fat as fuel instead carbohydrates to produce energy. The most challenging aspect of entering ketosis is consuming so many carbohydrates without knowing that they exist in the common healthy foods, such as fruits. The recommended amount of carbs is around 5% of our total calories.

4. You get way too hungry?

As your satiety off switch dims and your appetite spikes, every day becomes a battle; your body is unconsciously pushing you to eat, while you are desperately trying to resist. That is why as many as 95% of people who lose weight on conventional diets regain it all and often more within five years. According to a report from New England Journal of Medicine, ketosis is signalling the hypothalamus -the part of the brain that is active in hunger and controls the leptin which is a hormone acting mainly in the regulation of appetite and fat storage.

5. Having too much protein

Protein is great but having too much can cause problems because when your body digests it, a small amount of glucose is produced, this can reduce the body's ketosis potential. Glucose = sugar = carb. Remember to keep your protein consumption regulated with each meal and enjoy healthy fats, such as avocado, olive oil and nut butter.

6. Not eating enough fibre

Fibre is usually present in fruits and whole grains, but having too much of these can be detrimental. Be sure to have the right amount of fibre every day. You may feel hungry and overeat if you don’t eat enough fibre. The solution is to consume more vegetables, any green leaves are fine but if you don’t enjoy eating vegetables you can try fibre supplements.

7. Not enough vitamin B

When reducing carbs, you may feel tired and without energy due to the lack of B vitamins which are mostly found in whole grains and it will also slow down the weight loss process. We strongly advise eating meat, eggs and dairy products combined with leafy vegetables, broccoli, cauliflower, also with seeds and nuts.

8. fat controlling hormone, called leptin

Your body must generate the appropriate amount of leptin in order for the brain to recognize that you have eaten enough; otherwise, if your body releases more leptin than is needed, the brain would take longer to notice that you are eating, resulting in overeating.
